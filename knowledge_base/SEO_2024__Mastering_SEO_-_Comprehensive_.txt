Understanding & Addressing Issues With
Google Indexing
Have you noticed a sudden drop in your website's visibility
on Google? There are several reasons why Google might
stop indexing your website or deindexing specific pages.
Let's explore some common issues and solutions to ensure
your site stays in Google's good graces.
Dead Outbound Links:
Pages with dead outbound links can negatively impact your
site's indexation. Google sees these as a waste of its
crawling efforts. Regularly check and update your outbound
links to maintain a healthy linking profile.
Unwanted Video Links:
Including irrelevant or low-quality video links on your pages
may lead Google to consider them as non-useful content.
Ensure that your video content is relevant, high-quality, and
adds value to the user experience.
Excessive Call-to-Action Elements:
Pages with too many call buttons, WhatsApp links, or phone
call CTAs may be perceived as spammy. Google aims to
provide a smooth user experience, so avoid overloading
your pages with unnecessary call-to-action elements.
Non-Canonical URLs and Redirects:
Having variations of good pages, non-canonical URLs, or
excessive redirects can confuse Google's algorithms. Ensure
that your website has a clear URL structure, and implement
canonical tags to guide search engines to the preferred
version of a page.
Non-Content Pages:
Pages such as feeds, PDFs, or other non-content elements
might not contribute to Google's understanding of your site.
Focus on providing meaningful content and use proper tags
to differentiate non-content pages.
Understanding & Addressing Issues With
Google Indexing
Functional but Unhelpful Pages:
Some pages may be functional but don't assist Google in
understanding and indexing your site. Regularly review your
site structure and content to ensure every page serves a
purpose in enhancing user experience and search engine
visibility.
Changes in Website URLs:
If you've made changes to your website's URLs, ensure that
proper redirects are in place. Broken or missing redirects
can lead to deindexing and loss of ranking.
Dead Internal Linking:
Internal linking is crucial for SEO, but dead internal links can
harm your site's indexing and ranking. Periodically audit
your internal links to fix or remove any broken links.
Remember, maintaining a healthy and well-optimized
website is an ongoing process. Regularly monitor your site
for potential issues, and stay informed about SEO best
practices.

On-page SEO Elements in
WordPress 03
Header tags, also known as HTML heading tags, are an important
element in optimizing your website for search engines. They provide
structure and hierarchy to your content, helping search engines
understand the relevance and importance of each section. In this
tutorial, we will explore how to effectively use header tags for SEO
purposes.
HTML provides six levels of header tags, ranging from <h1> to <h6>.
The <h1> tag represents the highest level of importance, while the
<h6> tag represents the lowest. It is essential to use header tags in a
logical and hierarchical manner to improve the SEO ranking of your
website.
1. Utilize the <h1> Tag for the Main Heading: The <h1> tag should
be used only once on a page and should contain the main
keyword or key phrase you want to target. Search engines give
significant importance to the main heading, so ensure it
accurately reflects the content of the page.
2. Use Subheadings Effectively: Subheadings help break down
your content into smaller sections, making it easier for users to
navigate and understand. Include relevant keywords in
subheadings wherever appropriate. Use <h2> tags for the main
subheadings, <h3> tags for sub-subheadings, and so on, based on
the hierarchy of your content.
3. Maintain Consistency in Heading Levels: Maintaining
consistency in heading levels enhances the readability and
search engine optimization of your content. Avoid skipping
heading levels directly, such as going from <h2> to <h4>. Instead,
follow a logical progression and maintain a clear hierarchy.
4. Keep the Length of Headings Balanced: Try to keep your
headings concise and descriptive. While search engines
understand longer headings, excessively long headings can
affect the visual appeal of your content. Remember that header
tags are primarily meant to provide structure, so use them
wisely.
5. Incorporate Keywords Naturally: When including keywords in
your header tags, ensure they fit naturally within the context of
your content. Avoid keyword stuffing, as it can harm your SEO
efforts. Include keywords that are relevant to the content and
effectively convey the topic to both search engines and users.
6. Optimize Header Tags for Accessibility: Apart from SEO,
header tags also help improve accessibility for users with
disabilities who may be using screen readers. Ensure that your
headings accurately describe the content they precede and
provide a clear outline of the page when read sequentially.
7. Use Header Tags for Rich Snippets: Implementing header tags
correctly can enhance the appearance of your website's search

What is a URL?
A URL, or Uniform Resource Locator, is the address of a

of a webpage. Creating SEO-friendly URLs is essential for

engine results.
1. Keep URLs simple and descriptive:
-
An excellent SEO-friendly URL should be simple and
descriptive, incorporating relevant keywords that accurately
describe the webpage's content.
-
Avoid using long strings of numbers, symbols, or
meaningless characters in your URLs.
Instead, use hyphens to separate words, making it easier
-
for search engines and users to understand the URL.
Example:
Non-SEO friendly URL: www.example.com/page?p=123
SEO-friendly URL: www.example.com/digital-marketing-tips
2. Use relevant keywords in your URLs:
-
Including keywords in your URLs can help search engines
understand the relevance of your webpage to specific
search queries.
-
Research and identify relevant keywords related to your
content and incorporate them into your URL structure.
Example:
Non-SEO friendly URL:
www.example.com/products/category2/abc123
SEO-friendly URL: www.example.com/digital-marketing-
tools
3. Keep URLs short:
-
Shorter URLs are easier to read and remember for users, and
they also tend to perform better in search engine rankings.
-
Avoid unnecessary words, articles, and conjunctions in your
URLs.
Example:
Non-SEO friendly URL: www.example.com/this-is-a-long-
description-of-the-webpage
SEO-friendly URL: www.example.com/short-description
4. Use lowercase letters:
-
Always use lowercase letters in your URLs to maintain
consistency and avoid confusion.
-
Using uppercase letters may lead to duplicate content issues,
as search engines treat URLs with different capitalizations as
separate pages.
Example:
Non-SEO friendly URL: www.example.com/PRodUCTs
SEO-friendly URL: www.example.com/products
5. Use dashes instead of underscores or spaces:
-
When separating words in your URLs, use hyphens '-' instead
of underscores '_' or spaces.
Search engines prefer hyphens and are more easily readable
-
by users.
Example:
Non-SEO friendly URL: www.example.com/this_is_an_example
SEO-friendly URL: www.example.com/this-is-an-example
6. Avoid dynamic parameters in URLs:
-
Dynamic parameters, such as session IDs, query strings, or
random strings of characters, make your URLs look
complicated and can cause indexing issues.
-
Aim to have static URLs that are human-readable and
keyword-rich.
Example:
Non-SEO friendly URL: www.example.com/article?id=123
SEO-friendly URL: www.example.com/article/seo-best-
practices

-
If you have restructured your website or changed the URL of a
webpage, use 301 redirects to redirect users and search
engines from the old URL to the new one.
-
This will prevent broken links, maintain SEO value, and ensure
a smooth user experience.
Example:
Redirecting www.example.com/old-page to
www.example.com/new-page

Creating SEO-friendly URLs is essential to optimizing your

understandable, user-friendly, and search engine-friendly.

lowercase, and hyphenated. Implementing these best practices
will help improve your website's visibility and rankings in
search engine results.

to provide search engines with more detailed information about the
content on the page. This additional information helps search engines
understand the content better and display it in a more relevant way in
search results. Implementing schema markup on your website can
improve your search engine optimization (SEO) and make your pages
more appealing to users.
Why Use Schema Markup?
Schema markup makes it easier for search engines to understand the
content on your website, which can result in better rankings and more
targeted traffic. By providing search engines with structured data, you
are giving them more information about your content, such as the type
of content, the author, the date published, and more. This can help
search engines display your content in rich snippets, which are visually
more appealing and can lead to higher click-through rates.

SEO.
